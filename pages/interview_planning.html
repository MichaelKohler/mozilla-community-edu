Welcome to Week 2 - Interview Planning!

The field guide (sometimes called an interview guide or discussion guide) is a document that details what will happen in the interview. Creating this detailed plan is an essential preparatory step. The interviews themselves never happen as you imagine, but having a detailed plan prepares you to be flexible.

Introduction


Before you get started on this week's reading and activities, take a moment to consider our subject: "The Large Screen Experience". What did you learn about this subject during last week's secondary research? Did you focus primarily on technology and devices? What did you learn about people during that process?

Self-Assessment - Chapter 2



  1. What is the best mindset for interviewing people? What are ways to get into this mindset?
  2. What are the benefits of interviewing people where they live or work?
  3. What things should you, as an interviewer do to avoid negatively influence an interview?
  4. What ways can you think of that help you build a rapport with community members at Mozilla? How do you think those can help you when interviewing people you're interviewing?
  5. Have you noticed ways body language can impact a conversation, for example a conversation you recently had?
  6. In what, if any situation is it appropriate and constructive to share your own opinion or experience?
  7. Thinking about people in your life, what ways do you already use to help put people at ease ? How can you extend those same skills to interviewing?
